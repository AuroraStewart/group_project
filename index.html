First Year: Carrot and Coriander Soup

Ingredients for one big pot

  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 white onion, chopped
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 2 potatoes, chopped
  • 500g carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1.2l vegetable stock
  • handful of chopped coriander
  • salt & pepper to taste
  1. Heat up the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and sweat until soft.
  2. Stir in the coriander and the potatoes and cook for a few minutes.
  3. Add carrots and sweat everything for 2 more mintues.
  4. Fill up with stock until everything is well-covered. Let simmer covered for 25 minutes (until carrots are tender).
  5. Throw in the coriander and blend everything with a stick blender until soup is smooth.
  6. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Enjoy ;)
  • preparation time: 15min
  • cooking time: 25min

Bachelor: Spaghetti Bolognese

Ingredients for one big pot

  • 1tbsp vegetable oil
  • 500g beef mince
  • 1 white oninon, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 sticks of cellery, diced
  • 1tbsp tomato puree
  • 2 cans of tomatoes
  • 250ml beef stock
  • salt, pepper & sugar to taste
  • 1 pack of spaghetti
  • grated parmeggiano
  • 1 bunch of basil, chopped roughly
  1. Heat up the pan on medium heat. Add the oil to a pan and let it heat up properly. Test with a wooden spoon: if bubbles rise up around the spoon, the oil is hot enough!
  2. Add the mince and let it brown thoroughly. Add some salt and pepper to make the beef tasty!
  3. Add onions and garlic to the pan and let everything sweat until it is aromatic.
  4. Add carrots and cellery and let everything sweat. Add a little salt again at this stage to keep the flavour up.
  5. Make a little well in the center and add the tomatoe puree. Let it roast for a little while.
  6. Mix everything thoroughly and then add the tomatoes and beef stock to the pan. STir thoroughly to get all the aromatics from the bottom of the pan.
  7. Turn down the heat to low, cover the pan, and let everything simmer for 45-60 minutes. Stir occasionally!
  8. Taste the sauce and add salt, pepper and sugar to taste.
  9. Cook spaghetti according to the package until they are aldente. Save a little water to liquify the sauce a little bit.
  10. Drain pasta and add with some of the cooking water to the sauce, stir till combined well.
  11. Top with the chopped basil and parmeggiano to taste.
  12. Enjoy ;)
  • preparation time: 40min
  • cooking time: 1hr - 1.5hrs

Master: Asian Leek and Chicken Salad

Ingredients for 4 portions

  • 500g chicken breast filets
  • 3tbsp white wine
  • 3tsp corn starch
  • 1 egg white
  • 4 leeks
  • 6tbsp vegetable oil
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 50g sugar
  • 50g cashew nuts
  • salt & pepper to taste
  1. Pat dry the chicken and cut it in strips.
  2. Mix white wine, corn starch, and egg white in a bowl. Add the chicken, mix well, cover the bowl and let it rest in the fridge for 30 minutes.
  3. Wash the leeks and cut them in rings. Heat up 3tbsp of oil in a big pan, all the leeks and let them sweat for 10 minutes. Put it in a bowl to cool down.
  4. Combine soy sauce, sugar, and salt & pepper and mix the dressing with the leeks.
  5. Roast the cashew nuts in a pan with no oil till they are golden and aromatic. Set aside to cool.
  6. Heat up 3tbsp of oil in a pan and let it heat up. Fry the chicken breast til they are coloured on all sides.
  7. Let the meat cool down and add it to the leeks.
  8. Season with soy sauce to taste and sprinkle with the cashew nuts before serving.
  9. Enjoy ;)
  • preparation time: 20min
  • resting time: 30min
  • cooking time: 10min

Phd: Macarons

Ingredients for 20

  • 140g powdered sugar
  • 60g fine almond flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 egg whites (room temp)
  • 30g granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 drops food colouring
  • butter cream or ganache of choice
  1. Combine powdered sugar, almond flour and 1/2 tsp of salt, sift the mix through a fine-mesh sieve into a large bowl.
  2. In another bowl, beat egg whites and remaining salt with a hand mixer until you get soft peaks. Slowly add granulated suger, continue to beat till stiff peaks form.
  3. Add vanilla and beat till incorporated (not long at all!).If you want coloured macarons, add the colouring now.
  4. Add about 1/3 of the sifted almond flour to the egg whites and gently fold in with a spatula. continue to fold after the last addition until you can make a figure of an 8 while holding up the spatula.
  5. Transfer batter into poping bag with small round tip.
  6. Pipe the macarons onto the baking tray fitted with parchment paper in 3cm wide circles; space them at least 2cm apart! (Usually, you need 2-3 baking trays)
  7. Tap the baking tray on a flat surface 5 times to release air bubbles.
  8. Let macarons sit at room temperature for 45 minutes or until you can touch them without breaking the skin.
  9. preheat the oven to 150Â° fan.
  10. Bake macarons for 17-20 minutes or until feet are well-risen and macarons don't stick ot the paper anymore.
  11. transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  12. Add a little bit of your favourite butter cream or ganache onto one shell, top it with another shell to make a macaron.
  13. Place in an airtight containr for about 24h to make them taste their best!
  14. Enjoy ;)
  • preparation time: 20min
  • resting time: approx. 45min
  • baking time: 17min - 20 min
